<p align="justify">
I am a <a href="https://www.grappa.amsterdam/">GRAPPA</a> Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Amsterdam. <br>
<br>
I am a theoretical astrophysicist working at the interface of gravitational-wave astronomy, strong-field gravity, and dark matter phenomenology. My work focuses on using compact binary dynamics and gravitational-wave astronomy to probe black hole environments (including active galactic nuclei, accretion disks, and also dark matter structures). I am particularly interested in the interplay between physics beyond the standard model and gravity---this includes using gravitational waves to help us identify the nature of dark matter.<br>
  
Everything we know about dark matter comes from gravity. It is therefore natural to test dark matter where gravity is strongest: around black holes. <br>
<br>
Previously, I was a Juan de la Cierva Postdoctoral Fellow at the Theory division of the <a href="https://www.ifae.es/">Institute for High Energy Physics (IFAE)</a> of Barcelona, and before I did my PhD at the strong gravity group of <a href="https://centra.tecnico.ulisboa.pt/network/grit/">CENTRA-IST</a> in Lisbon.
</p>
